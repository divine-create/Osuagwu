About Evelyn

Where Heritage Meets Calling

Born on 29th March 1966 in Lagos, Ezinne Evelyn Iheoma Osuagwu is a trailblazer in Nigerian and global museology — known throughout her beloved Calabar as "Mama Museum." Her life is a testament to faith, discipline, and relentless dedication to Africa's cultural heritage.

The tenth and last-born child of Duru Felix and Ezinne Lolo Edna Adaku Okoro of Umuchima, Ihiagwa, Owerri West LGA, Imo State — Evelyn's journey from a family-filled home to the pinnacle of Nigeria's museum profession is nothing short of extraordinary. She holds a B.Ed in History Education (2.1) from the University of Jos, an MSc in Public Administration from the University of Calabar, and a Postgraduate Diploma in Museology (PGDM).

She began her career with the National Commission for Museums and Monuments (NCMM) as Curator I in Calabar in 1997. Over nearly three decades, she rose through every level — serving as Curator of the Slave History Museum, the Old Residency National Museum, and the National War Museum — before being appointed Director of Museums, NCMM on 1st January 2025.

A prolific scholar, she has authored over 26 articles in international and national journals, newspapers, exhibition brochures, and textbooks. She has curated landmark exhibitions including Man as an Object of Trade, Modern Day Slavery, and Peace and Unity.

Evelyn was born into a devoted Anglican family in the Church of Nigeria. Baptised as an infant and confirmed as a communicant in 1983, her faith has been the quiet, steady force behind every career milestone. Career Journey

From Curator I to Director of Museums

A remarkable 28-year journey through Nigeria's most significant cultural institutions.

1997
Curator I — NCMM Calabar
Began her distinguished career with the National Commission for Museums and Monuments. Quickly became known as "Mama Museum" across Calabar.
2017 – 2018
Curator — Slave History Museum, Calabar
Curated powerful exhibitions including Man as an Object of Trade and Modern Day Slavery at one of Africa's most important historical museums.
2018 – 2021
Curator — Old Residency National Museum, Calabar
Led one of Nigeria's most historically significant colonial-era museums, heading the Documentation Department, Gallery Unit, and Disciplinary Committee.
2021 – 2024
Curator — National War Museum
Stewarded Nigeria's premier military heritage institution, continuing her legacy of disciplined, scholarly museum leadership.
Feb 2024
Acting Director of Museums, NCMM
Appointed Acting Director — a recognition of her unmatched dedication, expertise, and leadership across Nigeria's museum network.
Crown Achievement
1st January 2025
Director of Museums
National Commission for Museums and Monuments
The culmination of 28 extraordinary years of service — appointed to the highest museum leadership position in Nigeria.
